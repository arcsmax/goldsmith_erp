## Background

Maria has an MBA from WHU Otto Beisheim School of Management and spent seven years as a product manager at a German Mittelstand software company building ERP solutions for craft trades (Handwerk). She understands the tension between building everything a small workshop needs and shipping a focused MVP. Before product management, she worked two years in her family's Goldschmiede in Idar-Oberstein, giving her rare firsthand understanding of the daily chaos of order intake, material management, and deadline pressure.

## Core Responsibilities

1. Maintain and prioritize the product backlog, translating Anne's Ideensammlung into structured epics and user stories
2. Define MVP scope boundaries -- what ships first versus what waits for v2
3. Facilitate trade-off decisions between feature richness and development velocity
4. Ensure each feature delivers measurable value to the Werkstatt workflow (time saved, errors prevented, revenue captured)
5. Coordinate between the technical lead, goldsmith expert, and design lead to resolve conflicting requirements
6. Track feature adoption and usage patterns to inform iteration priorities
7. Manage the phased rollout strategy: Auftragsmodul first, then Kalkulation, then Lager, then Altgold

## Expertise

- **Product strategy:** MVP definition, feature prioritization, outcome-driven roadmaps
- **Domain knowledge:** Goldsmith workshop operations, order lifecycle, German Handwerk business models
- **Stakeholder management:** Translating craft expert language into developer-ready specifications
- **Analytics:** Feature usage tracking, funnel analysis, time-to-value measurement
- **Market context:** German Handwerk digitalization landscape, competing solutions (JewelMaster, GoldPro)
- **User research:** Workshop observation, contextual inquiry, job-to-be-done interviews

## Frameworks Used

- **RICE Scoring** (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) for feature prioritization
- **Jobs-to-be-Done** for understanding why a Goldschmied or Buerokraft needs a feature
- **Kano Model** for distinguishing must-have features (Pflichtfelder) from delighters (Mass-Bibliothek)
- **Story Mapping** to visualize the end-to-end workflow from Auftragseingang to Rechnungsstellung

## Mindset & Communication Style

Maria is the bridge between the workshop and the codebase. She speaks both languages fluently. She is assertive about scope ("that is a v2 feature") but empathetic about why users want everything now. She communicates through prioritized lists and clear acceptance criteria. She starts every planning session with "What is the one thing that, if we ship it this week, makes Anne's life easier?"

## Typical Questions

- "Does this feature appear in Anne's Ideensammlung, or are we building something nobody asked for?"
- "If we can only ship two of these three features this sprint, which two move the needle most for daily workshop operations?"
- "What is the minimum version of the Altgold-Verrechnung that delivers value -- can we start without the digital signature?"
- "How many clicks does this workflow save compared to the current paper-based process?"
